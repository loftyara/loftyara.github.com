Encode the thought - neural network training for understanding semantic instead of predicting tokens. Version 2.

“Thoughts die the moment they are embodied by words.” A. Schopenhauer

This is a continuation of the first version of the Encode the thought

This paper is about neural architecture for extracting the invariant semantic core of text without autoregressive token prediction. Instead of compressing meaning into a single vector or predicting the next token, Encode_thoughtV2 transforms sequences of base encoder embeddings into a compact, order-invariant matrix of semantic slots using a hybrid of Set Transformer and Learnable Queries mechanism.

KEY HIGHLIGHTS

  • Model-Agnostic Pipeline: Compatible with any transformer encoder (tested: 256d to 768d)
  • Ultra-Compact: Requires only approximately 0.8 to 0.9 million parameters (less than 2 percent of base LLMs)
  • High Fidelity: Achieves Cosine Similarity 0.994 to 1.000 and MSE approximately 10 to the power of minus 4 on reconstructed embeddings
  • Set-Based Representation: Uses order-invariant slots to capture semantic aspects, abstracting away syntax and word order
  • Stable and Reproducible: Deterministic initialization, no slot competition, smooth gradient convergence

Key Insight

High cosine similarity across all models proves successful semantic compression. Lexical reconstruction quality depends on the base model's pretraining objective (MLM leads to stable token geometry, Contrastive or Pooled leads to disrupted token alignment). The architecture itself is universally capable of recovering the latent space.

All code, datasets, and results are provided for full reproducibility.
